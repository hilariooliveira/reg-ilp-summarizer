Two U.S. Air Force F-16 fighter jets crashed in the air today and exploded, an air force spokeswoman said.
A U.S. Air Force A-10 attack jet crashed and burned in Britain this morning while on a routine training flight.
West German police said one pilot was killed in the in-flight crash.
The Air Force spokeswoman, Capt. Gail Hayes, said the aircraft were on a training mission when they crashed near Bodenheim, about six miles south of Mainz.
The aircraft was attached the 48th Tactical Fighter Wing based at RAF Lakenheath Air Base in Britain.
The cause of the crash was not determined, officials said.
