The Second Amendment was, as was the rest of the first 10 amendments (the Bill of Rights), insisted upon by some of the states for their own protection from the federal government.
Didn't just about everyone own a gun in 1791?
This is not an argument regarding the merits of gun control laws.
The Second Amendment, in its language and its history, makes plain that the need for a well-regulated militia is a sufficient condition for the right to keep and bear arms.
The phrase "right of the people" appears four other times in the Bill of Rights (including the First Amendment).
