Here is a chronology of the Exxon Valdez oil spill, its cleanup and related developments:  March 24: The 987-foot tanker Exxon Valdez runs aground on Bligh Reef some 25 miles south of Valdez, Alaska, spilling nearly 11 million gallons of North Slope crude oil into Prince William Sound.
The Coast Guard said the slick and patches of oil separated from it were spread over an area of about 100 square miles.
Jan. 5: The Alaska Oil Spill Commission issues a report calling for an overhaul of the nation's oil transportation system.
Alaska Gov. Steve Cowper arrived in Valdez on Friday to evaluate the spill.
