Hurricane Gilbert battered the resorts of the Yucatan Peninsula today with 160 mph winds and torrential rains.
The hurricane killed at least five people Sunday in the Dominican Republic, civil defense officials said.
The storm was headed west northwest, said National Hurricane Center director Bob Sheets.
At 3 p.m. today, the center of the storm was near latitude 22.3 north and longitude 92.9 west, or about 380 miles southeast of Brownsville, Texas, the National Weather Service said.
The hurricane was moving toward the west northwest at 15 mph.
The hurricane center said Gilbert was the most intense storm on record in terms of barometric pressure.
